# Grade 2 Math – 1 Hour Lesson Plan  
**Curriculum Style:** Alberta Board of Education  
**Topic:** Two-Digit Addition (Without Regrouping)

---

# Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:

- Understand place value (tens and ones)
- Add two-digit numbers without regrouping
- Solve simple word problems
- Show their work using tens and ones

---

# Materials Needed

- Whiteboard or notebook  
- Pencil and eraser  
- Counters or blocks (optional)

---

# Lesson Plan (60 Minutes)

| Time | Activity |
|------|---------|
| 10 min | Warm-up counting |
| 15 min | Place value review |
| 15 min | Two-digit addition |
| 10 min | Practice questions |
| 10 min | Word problems |

---

# 1. Warm-Up (10 Minutes)

Count together from **1 to 100**.

Then ask students:

- What number comes after **29**?  
- What number comes before **50**?  
- What number comes after **67**?

---

# 2. Place Value Review (15 Minutes)

Explain that numbers have **tens and ones**.

Example:

**34**

| Tens | Ones |
|-----|-----|
| 3 | 4 |

Meaning:

3 tens = 30  
4 ones = 4  

Total = **34**

---

### Practice

Write the tens and ones.

| Number | Tens | Ones |
|------|------|------|
| 25 | ___ | ___ |
| 41 | ___ | ___ |
| 67 | ___ | ___ |

---

# 3. Two-Digit Addition (15 Minutes)

Explain that we **add ones first**, then **add tens**.

Example:

23 + 14

Ones:

3 + 4 = 7

Tens:

2 + 1 = 3

Answer = **37**

---

### Another Example

45 + 12

Ones:

5 + 2 = 7

Tens:

4 + 1 = 5

Answer = **57**

---

# 4. Practice Questions (10 Minutes)

Solve:

1. 21 + 16 = ______  
2. 34 + 12 = ______  
3. 46 + 13 = ______  
4. 52 + 17 = ______  

---

# 5. Word Problems (10 Minutes)

### Problem 1

Ravi has **23 apples**.  
His friend gives him **15 more apples**.

How many apples does Ravi have now?

23 + 15 = ______

---

### Problem 2

There are **32 birds** on a tree.  
**14 more birds** come.

How many birds are there now?

32 + 14 = ______

---

# Challenge Question

A shop has **41 candies**.  
The shopkeeper adds **18 more candies**.

How many candies are there in total?

41 + 18 = ______

---

# Wrap-Up

Ask students:

- What do we add first? (Ones)
- What do we add next? (Tens)

Example review:

27 + 11 = ?

Ones → 7 + 1 = 8  
Tens → 2 + 1 = 3  

Answer = **38**

---

# Homework

Solve:

1. 24 + 15 = ______  
2. 36 + 13 = ______  
3. 42 + 16 = ______  

Draw **tens and ones** to show your work.
