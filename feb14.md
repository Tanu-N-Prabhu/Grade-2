# Grade 2 English Language Arts (ELA)
**Alberta Board of Education**  
**Duration:** 1 Hour  
**Focus:** Writing – Descriptive Sentences

---

## Learning Objectives
Students will:
- Read a short model text
- Learn how describing words make writing better
- Write their own short descriptive sentences
- Share ideas clearly in writing

---

## Alberta ELA Outcomes (Grade 2)

- **1.1 Discover and Explore:** Respond to simple texts
- **2.2 Attend to Conventions:** Use describing words
- **3.1 Plan and Focus:** Write simple ideas
- **4.1 Enhance the Clarity of Communication:** Add details to writing

---

## Model Text

### *The Small Puppy*

I saw a small puppy at the park.  
The puppy had soft, brown fur.  
It had big, shiny eyes.  
The puppy ran on the green grass.  
It was playful and happy.

---

## Understanding Describing Words

Describing words tell us more about something.

Example from the story:

| Noun  | Describing Words |
|-------|------------------|
| puppy | small, soft |
| fur   | brown |
| eyes  | big, shiny |
| grass | green |

---

## Writing Activity

### Part 1: Add Describing Words

Fill in the blanks.

1. I saw a __________ cat.
2. The cat had __________ fur.
3. It sat on the __________ chair.

---

### Part 2: Write Your Own Description

Write 3–4 sentences about an animal.

Use describing words!

Sentence starters:

- I saw a __________.
- It had __________.
- It was __________.
- It liked to __________.

---

### Part 3: Make It Better

Add one describing word to each sentence.

1. The bird is flying.  
2. The dog is running.  
3. The fish is swimming.

---

## Optional Drawing

Draw your animal and label it with 2 describing words.

---

## Sharing

Students read one sentence aloud.

---

## Assessment (Informal)

- Use of describing words
- Completion of sentences
- Effort in writing

---

## Differentiation

- **Support:** Provide word bank  
  (soft, big, small, fast, happy, brown, fluffy)

- **Extension:** Write one extra sentence using two describing words
